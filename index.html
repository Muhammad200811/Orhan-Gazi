He is the son of Osman Gazi and Malhun Hatun , the founders of the Ottoman Principality . He is described as a tall, blue-eyed, revered monarch who is popular with the people, respects the ulema. He is often said to be involved in the public and listen to his troubles. Upon his father Osman Gazi's death, he became a bey in 1326. Orhan Bey was given the titles of Şücaeddin , "İhtiyareddin" and "Seyfeddin". There are also sources showing the date of death in 1359, 1360, 1361 and 1362.

The first period of Orhan Bey's principal years passed through conquests in Anatolia. During his principality, like all the other Anatolian principalities, he continued to pay İlhanlılar established in Iran and pay annual taxes. On the other hand, the Ottoman Principality gained more power with raids and conquests towards the Byzantine lands.

Orhan Bey conquered Mudanya in 1321 and brought his principality to the Marmara Sea coast. In 1323, Gebze built a mosque under its own name. Until 1321 and 1326, under the command of Gazi commanders, Ottoman Beyliks troops were dispatched to the Beylik borders; Konur Alp was conquered around the Western Black Sea,

In 1326, the target was the city ​​of Bursa , the largest center of the region and kept under blockade for years . First Orhaneli (then Atranos) fortress was taken and demolished; Afterwards, a headquarters was established in Pınarbaşı area to surround the Bursa fortress. However, as a result of diplomatic efforts of Köse Mihal Bey , the castle guard Evranos delivered the castle without war. A power of attorney gives Bursa its receipt as 2 Cemazievvel 726 (6 April 1326). However, some of the coin coins shown to be printed in Bursa on behalf of Orhan Gazi show earlier dates. In general, Ottoman historians state that Bursa was built by Orhan Gazi as the capital.
